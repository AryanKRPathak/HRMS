# 1. Policy Objective and Scope

## 1.1 Purpose

Keywords: attendance policy, work hours, employee attendance, office timings

The purpose of this policy is to define standardized attendance expectations, working-hour structures, shift guidelines, remote attendance rules, and operational compliance procedures for all employees.

This policy supports:

* operational continuity,
* workforce accountability,
* transparent attendance tracking,
* and fair attendance-related decision making.

---

## 1.2 Scope

Keywords: employee eligibility, permanent employees, remote employees

This policy applies to:

* full-time employees,
* probationary employees,
* hybrid workers,
* and approved remote employees.

The policy does not apply to:

* external contractors,
* interns,
* temporary consultants,
* or third-party vendor resources unless explicitly mentioned in their contracts.

---

## 1.3 Attendance Governance Principles

Keywords: attendance governance, HR compliance, attendance rules

The company follows the following attendance principles:

* accurate attendance tracking,
* operational transparency,
* manager accountability,
* employee flexibility within approved limits,
* and policy-driven attendance evaluations.

# 2. Standard Working Hours

---

## 2.1 General Office Timings

Keywords: office timing, work hours, shift timing

Standard office hours are:

Monday to Friday
09:30 AM – 06:30 PM IST

Total Working Duration:

* 9 hours including break time
* 8 hours of productive work expectation

---

## 2.2 Flexible Check-In Window

Keywords: flexible check-in, grace period, late arrival

Employees may check in between:
09:30 AM – 10:00 AM

Check-ins after 10:00 AM are categorized as late arrivals.

Repeated late arrivals may trigger:

* attendance warnings,
* manager review,
* or HR escalation workflows.

---

## 2.3 Remote & Hybrid Work Attendance

Keywords: remote attendance, hybrid work, work from home

Employees approved for remote or hybrid work arrangements must:

* check in through the HRMS portal,
* remain reachable during core business hours,
* maintain availability on Slack/Teams,
* and update work status appropriately.

---

## 2.4 Shift-Based Employees

Keywords: shift schedules, rotational shifts, operations team

Employees assigned to rotational or operational shifts must adhere to:

* assigned shift timing,
* handover procedures,
* and mandatory shift confirmation requirements.

Shift schedules are managed through the HR Operations Dashboard.

---

# 3. Attendance Tracking Rules

---

## 3.1 Check-In & Check-Out Requirements

Keywords: clock in, clock out, attendance logs

Employees must:

* record check-in at shift start,
* record check-out before leaving,
* and avoid proxy attendance activities.

Attendance logs are stored in the HRMS attendance ledger.

---

## 3.2 Late Arrival Classification

Keywords: late attendance, delayed check-in, attendance violation

Attendance classifications:

09:30 AM – 10:00 AM:

* Within grace window

10:01 AM – 11:00 AM:

* Marked as Late Entry

After 11:00 AM:

* Eligible for Half-Day Attendance classification

Repeated late entries exceeding:

* 5 occurrences within 30 days
  may trigger manager escalation.

---

## 3.3 Missing Check-Out Rules

Keywords: missing checkout, attendance anomaly, incomplete logs

Employees failing to complete check-out logs may receive:

* provisional attendance status,
* pending verification notifications,
* or HR review requests.

Employees must regularize missing logs within:
48 working hours.

---

## 3.4 Half-Day Attendance Rules

Keywords: half-day attendance, partial attendance

Employees may be marked as Half-Day under the following conditions:

* check-in after 11:00 AM,
* work duration below 4.5 hours,
* or unauthorized extended inactivity.

Managers retain authority to override attendance classifications in exceptional situations.

---

## 3.5 Attendance Regularization Requests

Keywords: attendance correction, regularization request

Employees may submit attendance correction requests for:

* biometric failures,
* network outages,
* emergency disruptions,
* or operational travel.

All requests require:

* reason submission,
* manager review,
* and supporting documentation where necessary.

---

---

## 4.1 Overtime Authorization

Keywords: overtime approval, extended work hours

Overtime work must receive:

* and HR logging approval.

Unauthorized overtime is not eligible for compensatory benefits.

## 4.2 Weekend Work & Compensatory Off

Employees working on:

* weekends,
* company holidays,
* or emergency operational shifts
  may qualify for Comp-Off eligibility.

Comp-Off requests must be submitted within:
7 working days.

The company reserves the right to:

* audit attendance records,

## 7.3 Continuous Attendance Violations

* formal warnings,

# 8. Exceptions & Edge Cases

---

## 8.1 Emergency Situations

Keywords: emergency attendance, crisis situations

Attendance exceptions may be granted during:

* medical emergencies,
* severe weather conditions,
* civil disruptions,
* or infrastructure outages.

Employees must notify managers as early as possible.

---

## 8.2 Probationary Employees

Keywords: probation attendance, probation rules

Probationary employees are expected to maintain stricter attendance compliance standards during evaluation periods.

Repeated attendance issues during probation may affect confirmation decisions.

---

## 8.3 System Downtime & Technical Failures

Keywords: HRMS downtime, technical issue, biometric failure

If attendance systems become unavailable:

* employees must notify reporting managers,
* maintain manual attendance evidence,
* and submit regularization requests after restoration.

# Common Employee Questions

Q: What happens if I check in after 10:00 AM?
A: Your attendance may be marked as a Late Entry depending on your check-in time.

Q: When am I marked Half-Day?
A: Employees checking in after 11:00 AM or working less than 4.5 hours may be marked Half-Day.

Q: Can I regularize missing attendance?
A: Yes. Employees can submit attendance correction requests within 48 working hours.

Q: Do remote employees need attendance tracking?
A: Yes. Remote employees must check in through the HRMS portal and remain operationally available.

Q: Can overtime be claimed without approval?
A: No. Overtime requires prior manager authorization.
