# 1. Policy Objective and Scope

## 1.1 Purpose

Keywords: policy purpose, leave rules, HR policy, employee leave guidelines

The purpose of this policy is to establish clear, robust guidelines governing leave entitlements, accruals, approval workflows, and administrative practices. This policy ensures high availability for client deliverables, maintains compliance with relevant labor regulations, and supports a healthy work-life balance for all personnel.

---

## 1.2 Scope

Keywords: employee eligibility, permanent employees, interns, contractors

This policy applies to all full-time and part-time permanent employees on the payroll of the India entity of the company. It does not apply to short-term interns, independent contractors, or freelancers, whose terms are governed strictly by their respective consulting agreements.

---

## 1.3 Compliance Context

Keywords: labor laws, maternity act, India HR compliance

This policy is drafted to comply with the state-specific Shops and Establishments Act, the Maternity Benefit Act (Amendment) 2017, and other applicable national labor regulations in India.

---

# 2. Types of Leave and Entitlements

All permanent employees are entitled to a total of 28 days of paid leave per calendar year.

---

## 2.1 Privilege Leave (PL) / Earned Leave (EL)

Keywords: earned leave, privilege leave, vacation leave, paid leave

Entitlement: 18 calendar days per annum.
Accrual: Credited monthly at a rate of 1.5 days for every completed month of service.
Purpose: Scheduled personal travel, extended vacations, or personal projects.
Minimum Usage: Cannot be taken for durations of less than 2 consecutive working days per request.

---

## 2.2 Casual Leave (CL) & Sick Leave (SL)

Keywords: casual leave, sick leave, emergency leave, medical leave, leave balance

Entitlement: 10 calendar days per annum (combined CL/SL pool).
Accrual: Credited upfront on January 1st of each calendar year. For mid-year joiners, the credit is pro-rated.

Purpose:

* CL: Short-term urgent personal matters, unexpected errands, or family obligations.
* SL: Recovery from unexpected illnesses, medical emergencies, or diagnostic procedures.

Maximum Usage:
No more than 3 consecutive CL/SL days may be taken without medical validation.

---

## 2.3 Maternity Leave (ML)

Keywords: maternity leave, pregnancy leave, women employees

Entitlement: 26 weeks (182 calendar days) of fully paid leave.

Eligibility:
Women employees who have completed at least 80 days of active service in the 12 months immediately preceding the expected delivery date.

Documentation:
A valid medical certificate from a registered obstetrician/gynecologist is mandatory.

---

## 2.4 Paternity Leave (PTL)

Keywords: paternity leave, adoption leave, parental leave

Entitlement: 10 consecutive working days of fully paid leave.

Eligibility:
Male employees on the birth or legal adoption of a child.

Window:
Must be availed within 6 months of the child's birth or legal adoption date.

---

## 2.5 Bereavement Leave

Keywords: bereavement, family emergency, death leave

Entitlement: 5 consecutive working days.

Applies only to immediate family members.

---

## 2.6 Compensatory Off (Comp-Off)

Keywords: compensatory off, overtime leave, weekend work

Working on weekends or gazetted holidays requires prior written authorization from the reporting authority.

Comp-Off must be consumed within 60 calendar days.

---

## 2.7 Loss of Pay (LOP) / Unpaid Leave

Keywords: unpaid leave, LOP, leave exhaustion

LOP is considered an exceptional status when employees exhaust all available leave balances.

Requires approval from:

* Department Head
* HR Head

---

# 3. Operational Rules and Accruals

---

## 3.1 Accrual Cycle

Keywords: leave accrual, joining date, leave cycle

The leave cycle follows the calendar year from January 1 to December 31.

Joining date between 1st–15th:

* Full accrual credited

Joining date after 15th:

* Half accrual credited for first month

---

## 3.2 Carry Forward and Encashment Caps

Keywords: carry forward, leave encashment, leave expiry

Privilege Leave:
Maximum 12 unused PL days may be carried forward.

Casual/Sick Leave:
Unused CL/SL balances lapse on December 31st and cannot be carried forward.

PL Encashment:
Up to 30 days can be encashed during employee exit.

---

## 3.3 Half-Day Rules

Keywords: half-day leave, first half leave, second half leave

CL and SL may be requested as half-day leaves.

PL cannot be processed for half-day increments.

---

# 4. Leave Request and Approval Workflows

Keywords: approval workflow, escalation, leave approval SLA

[Employee Submits Request in Portal]
↓
Notification Sent to Reporting Manager
↓
Manager Review (48-Hour SLA)
↓
Approved → HR Records Updated
OR
No Action / Rejected → Auto Escalation to Department Head / HR

---

## 4.1 Notification Timelines

Keywords: planned leave, emergency leave, leave notice

Planned Leave (2–5 Days):

* Request at least 7 working days in advance

Extended Leave (>5 Days):

* Request at least 21 working days in advance

Emergency Sickness:

* Notify reporting manager before 10:00 AM

---

## 4.2 System Submission Requirements

Keywords: HR portal, leave submission, delegate assignment

All leave requests must be submitted through the HR portal with:

* date range
* backup contact number
* delegate assignment

---

## 4.3 Approval Hierarchy and SLA

Keywords: manager approval, escalation, SLA

Managers must review requests within 48 hours.

If unresolved:

* auto-escalation triggers to Department Head.

---

# 5. Employee Responsibilities

---

## 5.1 Handover and Business Continuity

Keywords: transition notes, project handover, OOO responder

Employees taking leave longer than 3 days must:

* provide transition notes
* assign operational backup
* configure out-of-office responders

---

## 5.2 Mandatory Contact Details

Keywords: emergency contact, leave communication

Employees on leave must provide emergency contact details for urgent operational situations.

---

## 5.3 Retroactive Submissions

Keywords: retroactive leave, emergency absence

Emergency CL/SL requests must be logged within 3 working days after return.

---

# 6. Confidentiality, HR Compliance, and Escalations

---

## 6.1 Unexcused Absence / Absconding Clause

Keywords: unapproved absence, abandonment of service

Absence exceeding 5 consecutive working days without approval triggers HR investigation.

---

## 6.2 Medical Certification Audits

Keywords: medical proof, sick leave audit

Medical certificates may be requested for:

* sick leave longer than 3 days
* leaves around holidays or delivery windows

---

## 6.3 Executive Override and Grievance Redressal

Keywords: HR appeal, grievance escalation

Employees may escalate rejected leave requests to the HR Head.

---

# 7. Exceptions and Edge Cases

---

## 7.1 Probationary Period Rules

Keywords: probation leave, new employees

Employees under probation:

* do not accrue PL
* receive limited CL/SL eligibility

---

## 7.2 Compassionate / Force Majeure Extensions

Keywords: disaster leave, emergency extension

Employees affected by disasters or major emergencies may request advance leave allocation.

---

## 7.3 Intervening Holidays (Sandwich Rule)

Keywords: sandwich rule, weekend leave calculation

The company does not enforce a strict sandwich rule.

Example:
If an employee takes leave on Friday and Monday, the weekend is excluded from leave deduction.

# Common Employee Questions

Q: How many casual leaves do I get annually?
A: Employees receive 10 combined CL/SL days annually.

Q: Can I carry forward casual leave?
A: No. Casual/Sick Leave balances lapse at the end of the calendar year.

Q: Can I take half-day Privilege Leave?
A: No. PL cannot be processed in half-day increments.

Q: What happens if my manager does not approve leave within 48 hours?
A: The request automatically escalates to the Department Head.

Q: Can employees on probation take Privilege Leave?
A: No. Employees under probation do not accrue PL.
