# 1. Policy Objective and Scope

## 1.1 Purpose

Keywords: payroll policy, salary processing, reimbursements, compensation

The purpose of this policy is to define standardized payroll processing procedures, salary component structures, reimbursement workflows, and access-control rules for compensation-related information.

This policy ensures:

* accurate salary processing,
* reimbursement transparency,
* enterprise-grade payroll confidentiality,
* and operational compliance with Indian labor and tax regulations.

---

## 1.2 Scope

Keywords: employee eligibility, payroll applicability

This policy applies to:

* full-time permanent employees,
* probationary employees,
* and approved payroll-eligible staff.

The policy does not apply to:

* contractors,
* freelance consultants,
* interns,
* or external vendors unless explicitly mentioned in contractual agreements.

---

## 1.3 Payroll Governance Principles

Keywords: payroll governance, salary confidentiality, payroll compliance

The company follows:

* restricted payroll visibility,
* finance-approved payroll processing,
* role-based payroll access,
* audit-based payroll verification,
* and approval-driven compensation workflows.

Sensitive payroll information is protected through:

* field-level access restrictions,
* masked banking details,
* and role-specific visibility boundaries.

---

# 2. Payroll Cycle & Salary Processing

---

## 2.1 Monthly Payroll Schedule

Keywords: payroll cycle, salary credit date, payroll release

Payroll is processed on a monthly basis.

Salary processing timeline:

* Payroll Lock Date: 25th of every month
* Finance Approval Window: 26th–28th
* Salary Credit Date: Last working day of the month

If the final working day falls on:

* weekends,
* public holidays,
* or banking outages,
  salary may be processed on the previous business day.

---

## 2.2 Payroll Inputs & Validation

Keywords: payroll inputs, attendance validation, salary processing

Payroll calculations are generated using:

* attendance records,
* approved leave data,
* reimbursement approvals,
* overtime eligibility,
* and statutory deduction rules.

Payroll cannot be finalized until:

* attendance discrepancies,
* unresolved leave conflicts,
* and pending reimbursement validations
  are resolved.

---

## 2.3 Payroll Locking Rules

Keywords: payroll lock, payroll approval, finance controls

Once payroll is finalized and locked:

* salary components cannot be modified,
* reimbursement edits are disabled,
* and payroll records become audit-protected.

Corrections after payroll lock require:

* HR approval,
* Finance approval,
* and payroll adjustment processing.

---

# 3. Salary Structure & Compensation Components

---

## 3.1 Salary Components

Keywords: salary breakdown, compensation structure, CTC

Employee compensation may include:

* Basic Salary
* House Rent Allowance (HRA)
* Special Allowance
* Performance Bonus
* Internet Allowance
* Meal Allowance
* Employer PF Contributions

Salary structures are defined during onboarding and maintained in the HRMS compensation module.

---

## 3.2 Salary Deductions

Keywords: salary deduction, tax deduction, professional tax

Deductions may include:

* Provident Fund (PF)
* Professional Tax
* Income Tax (TDS)
* Leave Without Pay (LOP)
* Recovery Adjustments
* Insurance Contributions

AI HR Copilot may explain deductions but cannot modify payroll calculations.

---

## 3.3 Bonus & Incentive Processing

Keywords: bonuses, incentives, performance payouts

Performance bonuses and incentives:

* require management approval,
* may vary by department,
* and are processed separately from fixed compensation.

Bonus payouts are not guaranteed unless formally documented in compensation agreements.

## 4.1 Eligible Reimbursements

Keywords: reimbursements, travel claims, internet reimbursement

Eligible reimbursement categories include:

* travel expenses,
* internet reimbursement,
* work-from-home equipment,

* and approved operational expenses.

## 4.2 Reimbursement Submission Workflow

Keywords: reimbursement workflow, expense claims

Employees must:

* upload invoices,
* provide claim descriptions,
* and submit reimbursement requests through the HRMS portal.

Workflow:

[Employee Submits Claim]
↓
Manager Verification
↓
Finance Review
↓
Approved → Added to Payroll Cycle

---

## 4.3 Reimbursement Processing SLA

Keywords: reimbursement timeline, reimbursement delay

Approved reimbursements are generally processed within:

* 7–14 working days

Delayed or disputed reimbursements may trigger:

* finance escalation workflows,
* additional document requests,
* or HR review.

# 5. Payroll Access Control & Data Security

---

## 5.1 Role-Based Payroll Visibility

Keywords: payroll RBAC, salary access, payroll permissions

Payroll visibility follows strict role-based access controls.

Access levels:

Employee:

* Self payroll only

HR Associate:

* Restricted operational visibility
* No full compensation editing access

HR Head:

* Department-wide payroll visibility

Admin:

* Full payroll administration access

---

## 5.2 Bank Account & Sensitive Data Protection

Keywords: bank masking, sensitive payroll data

Sensitive payroll information including:

* bank account numbers,
* routing details,
* PAN information,
* and salary structures
  is protected using:
* masked display layers,
* restricted API authorization,
* and field-level visibility rules.

Unauthorized payroll access attempts are logged and audited.

---

## 5.3 Payroll Audit Logging

Keywords: payroll audits, audit trails

The HRMS platform maintains audit logs for:

* salary changes,
* reimbursement edits,
* payroll approvals,
* bank detail modifications,
* and payroll exports.

Audit logs include:

* timestamp,
* acting user,
* modified fields,
* and approval history.

## 6.2 AI Safety Boundaries

Keywords: AI restrictions, payroll AI limitations

AI HR Copilot cannot:

* modify salary records,
* approve reimbursements,
* finalize payroll,
* expose payroll data of other employees,

Sensitive payroll actions always require human authorization.

* or reimbursement conflicts

# Common Employee Questions

Q: Why was my net salary lower this month?
A: Salary changes may occur due to tax deductions, unpaid leave, or reimbursement adjustments.

Q: Can HR Associates view my bank account details?
A: No. Sensitive banking information is protected through role-based access controls.

Q: How long do reimbursements take to process?
A: Approved reimbursements are typically processed within 7–14 working days.

Q: Can AI HR Copilot change payroll records?
A: No. AI HR Copilot cannot modify payroll data or approve salary changes.

Q: What happens after payroll is locked?
A: Payroll edits are restricted and corrections require approval workflows.
